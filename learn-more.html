What is MetaOSINT?

MetaOSINT is a free and openly available aggregation of "top" tools & resources intended to help jumpstart OSINT investigations. MetaOSINT was built by a former OSINT analyst & investigator to help answer questions like "I wonder if thereâ€™s a way to find __ specific piece of information online?"

By compiling and prioritizing among thousands of tools and resources, MetaOSINT seeks to help OSINT practitioners intuitively surface the most relevant resources much more quickly. MetaOSINT was published in June 2021 and received major updates around its first anniversary.

Methodology

MetaOSINT v2 contains resources from three source buckets, which vary in confidence level and potential bias:

  • MetaOSINT: In 2021, the project's author conducted a survey of nearly 30 lists of OSINT tools & resources, with a goal of identifying overlapping concentrations of tools. The lists contained a staggering 17,000 source links, 11,000 of which were unique. The v1 MetaOSINT dataset highlighted 4,800+ of these resources cited multiple times across the lists, as well as a few hundred more cited once and with some discernible topic/categorization. Resources derived from this bucket contain a citation count in the table feature.
  • Social Share: v2 introduces mechanisms to poll social media platforms for potentially relevant resources as a way of continuing to expand the dataset. There is some potential for noise/false positives/less-relevant results here, but initial test sets have provided a useful supplement to the v1 data.
  • Manual: To fill remaining data gaps, a small number of resources will be added manually.
